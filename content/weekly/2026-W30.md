### [Europe is at risk of creating a human rights black hole with its ‘return hubs’ for migrants | Michael O'Flaherty](https://www.theguardian.com/commentisfree/2026/jul/20/europe-risk-human-rights-black-hole-return-hubs-migrants)

- 歐盟即將通過一項新法規，允許成員國設立離岸「遣返中心」以處理和驅逐移民，目前已有19個成員國支持迅速實施。此舉將移民管理外包，引發了重大人權疑慮，因為澳洲、美國等國的類似計畫曾導致任意拘留與不人道待遇等嚴重問題。歐洲委員會人權專員等批評者警告，若缺乏嚴格的法律保障與獨立監督，這些中心恐將成為規避司法管轄的「人權黑洞」。
- The EU is finalizing a regulation to create offshore 'return hubs' for processing and deporting migrants, with 19 member states eager to implement the policy. This move towards outsourced migration management raises significant human rights concerns, as similar schemes by Australia, the US, and the UK have historically resulted in arbitrary detention and inhumane conditions. Critics, including the Council of Europe's human rights commissioner, warn these hubs could become 'human rights black holes' without strict, legally binding safeguards and independent oversight.
