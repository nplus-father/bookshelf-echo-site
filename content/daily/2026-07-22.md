### [Ads call ovarian cancer a ‘silent killer’. But does urging women to undergo early detection testing do more harm than good? | Antiviral](https://www.theguardian.com/commentisfree/2026/jul/22/ovarian-cancer-early-detection-testing-ads)

- 一家澳洲公司正直接向消費者行銷一款要價1495澳元、基於機器學習的卵巢癌早期血液檢測，並使用「無聲殺手」等引發恐懼的宣傳詞。然而，癌症專家警告，該測試缺乏同儕審查數據且未獲監管機構批准，其94%的特異度在卵巢癌這種罕見疾病上會產生大量偽陽性結果，可能每23個真實病例就伴隨著近6000個偽陽性。此案例凸顯了在未經充分臨床驗證下部署醫療AI的重大風險，不當的統計應用和激進的行銷策略可能導致患者焦慮、不必要的醫療程序與傷害。
- An Australian company is marketing a $1,495 machine learning-based blood test for early ovarian cancer detection directly to consumers, using fear-based language like "silent killer." However, cancer experts warn the test has limited peer-reviewed data, is not regulator-approved, and its 94% specificity would generate a massive number of false positives for a rare disease—potentially creating nearly 6,000 false positives for every 23 true cases in a large cohort. This case highlights the significant risks of deploying unproven medical AI, where flawed statistical application and aggressive marketing can lead to patient harm, anxiety, and unnecessary medical procedures.
